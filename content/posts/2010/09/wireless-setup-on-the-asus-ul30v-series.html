--- title: Wireless Setup on the ASUS UL30V Series created: !!timestamp '2010-09-18 00:00:00' tags: - fedora - linux - wireless --- {% mark excerpt -%} I recently purchased an ASUS UL30V laptop, 13.3" machine. For some oddball reason the wireless rate defaulted to 1mb/s, which makes for an awful online experience. How did I find this? Took a minute to figure out what was going on with this, but looking closely at the output of the iwconfig command showed me "Bit Rate=54 Mb/s" for the wlan0 device. A quick fix. {% endmark -%} Run: iwconfig wlan0 rate 54M [/cc] This took care of the problem and cleared my connection. But wait.... when I reboot the rate resets to 1Mb/s again. The previous command was not persistent through a reboot, therefore I would have to run this command everytime I connect. Thank God for simple automation though. I added the following lines to my **/etc/rc.local** file. ifconfig wlan0 up iwconfig wlan0 rate 54M And wabam! Everytime I boot up this sweet little machine, I connect with normal speeds.
